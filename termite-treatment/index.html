Common Signs You Might Need Termite Treatment in Des Plaines, IL

Recognizing the early signs of a termite infestation is crucial for preventing extensive damage. While these pests are often hidden, there are several indicators that you may have a problem. Ignoring these signs can lead to significant structural issues and costly repairs down the road. It's important to be vigilant and contact a professional if you notice any of these warning signs.

Mud Tubes on Walls

Subterranean termites build narrow mud tubes, often the width of a pencil, on exterior or interior walls. These tubes serve as protected pathways for termites to travel between their underground colony and their food source (your home's wood). If you discover these mud tubes, resist the urge to break them open. Doing so might cause the termites to abandon the area, making it harder for us to locate the colony and assess the full extent of the infestation. It's best to leave them undisturbed and contact us immediately for a professional evaluation of your termite situation in Des Plaines, IL.

Damaged Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, leaving a thin layer of wood or paint intact. This can make the damage difficult to spot initially. Tapping on wood and hearing a hollow sound can indicate termite activity. You might also see blistering or darkening of the wood. Termite damage differs from other wood-boring pests and distinguishing between them is important for effective treatment. For instance, carpenter ants, while also damaging wood, create galleries for nesting, not for consumption. Our experts can accurately diagnose the type of wood damage and the pest responsible.

Discarded Wings

Swarming termites, which are reproductive adults leaving the colony to establish new ones, shed their wings after finding a suitable location. You might find piles of these discarded wings near windowsills, doors, or around light sources. These tiny, translucent wings are a clear indicator of a recent termite swarm and the potential for a nearby active colony. Swarming typically occurs during warmer months, often after rainfall, but can happen at other times depending on the species and environmental conditions. Finding these wings inside your home is a strong signal that a colony is present within the structure itself.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites, unlike subterranean termites, do not build mud tubes. Instead, they bore directly into the wood and create small kick-out holes through which they expel their fecal pellets, known as frass. Frass resembles tiny, granular pellets and is often found in small piles near infested wood. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood the termites are consuming. Finding piles of what looks like sawdust or pepper near wooden structures is a strong sign of a drywood termite infestation. Prompt action is needed to prevent further damage caused by these insidious pests in your Des Plaines property.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

Severe termite infestations can compromise the structural integrity of your home, leading to sagging floors, ceilings, or walls. While other issues can cause structural problems, termite damage should always be considered as a possibility if these signs appear. This level of damage indicates a long-standing and extensive infestation that requires immediate professional intervention. The longer the infestation is left untreated, the more severe and costly the repairs will become. Our team can assess the extent of the structural damage and recommend appropriate treatment strategies to stop the termites from causing further harm.

Pinpoint Holes in Drywall

Termites can also feed on the paper coating of drywall. Tiny, pinpoint holes in drywall or bubbling paint can be a sign of their presence underneath. These smaller signs are often overlooked but can be early indicators of a developing problem. Do not dismiss these seemingly minor details. Investigating these small holes or paint irregularities can help in early detection of a termite problem, which is crucial for minimizing damage and treatment costs. We can help you identify whether these signs are indicative of termite activity or other issues, such as moisture problems.

Visible Swarms

Seeing a swarm of winged insects emerging from your home or yard is a definitive sign of a termite infestation. Swarming is the reproductive stage of termites and indicates that a mature colony is present nearby. While swarms themselves may be short-lived, their presence is a clear call for action. Documenting the sighting, even taking a picture or collecting a sample, can be helpful for our identification process. While seeing a swarm can be alarming, it provides a valuable opportunity for us to accurately diagnose the problem and implement an effective treatment plan for your Des Plaines, IL property. You might also need bee and wasp removal if you're seeing other types of flying insects.

Wood that Sounds Hollow When Tapped

As termites consume the interior of wooden structures, the wood becomes hollow. Tapping on affected areas with a screwdriver or other tool can reveal this hollowness. This technique, often used in inspections, can help pinpoint areas of suspected termite activity even if visually there are no obvious signs. It provides a tactile indicator of internal damage. Different types of wood will produce slightly different sounds when healthy versus hollowed out by termites. Our experienced technicians know what to listen for when conducting inspections. This is a simple yet effective method you can use as a preliminary check if you suspect termite activity.

Stuck Windows or Doors

When termites tunnel through wooden window or door frames, their activity and the resulting damage can cause the wood to warp and swell, making windows and doors difficult to open or close. This problem is often attributed to humidity or settling, but it could be a sign of a hidden termite issue. Before assuming it's a weather-related issue, especially if it's a persistent problem or coincides with other signs, consider the possibility of termites. Addressing this type of issue promptly is important not just for functionality but also to prevent further structural compromise. Our inspection can help determine if termites are the root cause of your sticky windows or doors in Des Plaines.

Subterranean Termite Shelter Tubes

Sometimes, subterranean termites build their mud tubes in less obvious locations, such as crawl spaces, basements, or even within walls. These tubes are essential for them to maintain the high humidity levels needed for their survival while traveling from their colony to food sources. Finding these tubes in these hidden areas provides concrete evidence of a subterranean termite infestation and indicates where the main activity pathways are located. Probing for these tubes in less visible areas is a critical part of a thorough professional termite inspection, ensuring no areas are missed. These tubes can be difficult to spot without proper tools and knowledge, highlighting the importance of professional inspection services.

Drywood Termite Kick-Out Holes

In addition to frass, identifying the small ""kick-out"" holes that drywood termites create to expel their droppings is another key indicator. These holes are typically tiny, often less than 1 millimeter in diameter, and can be difficult to spot without close inspection. The presence of multiple kick-out holes suggests significant activity within the wooden structure. These tiny details can make a big difference in diagnosing and treating a drywood termite infestation. Knowing where to look for these subtle signs is part of the expertise our technicians bring to every service call in Des Plaines. If you see very fine, almost powdery residue beneath or around wooden furniture or structures, it's worth investigating for these holes.

Damage Resembling Water Damage

Sometimes, advanced termite damage can resemble water damage, causing wood to look warped, discolored, or stained. It can be easy to confuse the two, but proper diagnosis is essential for effective treatment. Unlike water damage which is usually localized to areas with leaks, termite damage can be more widespread and follow specific pathways within the wood structure. Misdiagnosing termite damage as water damage can lead to ineffective repairs and allow the infestation to continue causing harm. Our professionals are trained to distinguish between different types of damage to ensure the correct problem is addressed. Getting a professional assessment if you suspect either issue is always the safest approach.

Understanding the Severity of the Threat

The damage often occurs in hidden areas within walls, under floors, and in attics, making it difficult to detect until the problem is severe. Being aware of the potential severity emphasizes the importance of professional inspection and treatment. Don't underestimate the destructive capability of these tiny insects when they work together in large numbers within a colony structure designed for efficiency and constant wood consumption. Consider the value of your property and the peace of mind that comes with knowing it is protected from these silent destroyers.
